Sierra Leone Report IV

Home / Sierra Leone Report IV

Director Jeremy Bristow reports from Bombeh, Sierra Leone, where he is filming the Ebola outbreak on behalf of Make Waves.

Returned to 34 Bombeh road today.

Filmed sprayers getting into body suits. Chlorine mix being made and put into spray tanks. They started to spray the ground around the house.  The quarantine family watched from the veranda as sprayers spray steps and posts and move inside.

A large crowd is gathered round from all sides of the house.  Médecins Sans Frontiers addresses people and the crowd.  This is serious – stand back.

One of the Ebola suspects, a man of around 50 walks and sits in the back of the ambulance.  From a safe distance the medics start ask him questions and fill in notes.  The rest of the family look on. Sprayers are walking past.

Then he gets into the ambulance.

Then a man walks out holding a tiny two year-old.  They sit on the veranda and are asked questions.  They then climb in to ambulance.  The psycho-social counsellor the addresses ambulance driver. One of these men is very sick ‘please, please’ drive very slow.

Ambulance drives off into distance.

At the hospital

Filmed ambulance sitting waiting at hospital, one door open – just one foot poignantly sticks out.

Eventually two bio-dressed people in full protective clothing outfits get out and walk up to them.  50 year old gets out and walks down path towards the Isolation Centre. Man in Bio-body suit takes the little girl by her hand.  She looks up to him as they walk.

One of the earlier children, a girl, sits out on a plastic seat.  Later joined by a boy – from another family.

Inside the unit is dark.  People in bio-suits sweeping and mopping etc.

Another ambulance arrives with a young teenage girl and a 5 year-old boy get out and are walked to the isolation ward.

Cold blooded Dan averages around 36.0.

Me, I vary between 36.5 and 36.9 using the ear thermometer.   This morning I took three reading is 5 minutes. 36.7/36.9/36.8.
I think normal is meant to be 37.3.

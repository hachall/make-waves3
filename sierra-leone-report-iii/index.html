Sierra Leone Report III

Home / News / Sierra Leone Report III

Director Jeremy Bristow reports from Sierra Leone, where he is filming the Ebola outbreak on behalf of Make Productions.

We obtained a couple of good sequences today.  The best being Bo’s very charismatic psycho-social counsellor, addressing an extended family who have been quarantined. Ten of them,  all sitting on the stone veranda of their very sparse house, in a muddy street, as people from neighbouring households look on.   Kalashnikov-bearing soldiers and police stand ready.

The family have lost between 4 and  6 to Ebola, three more are in the local hospital’s isolation unit, if they’re positive they will go to Médecins Sans Frontiers to cared for by the nurse.

The counsellor goes through the whole gamut of the Ebola story with verve.  She tries to persuade them not to be afraid of going to the treatment centre.

Tomorrow the spray team will go to the house to disinfect everything. If one of the three family members is positive, they will go to Médecins Sans Frontiers.  If they recover, the counsellor will re-integrate them into the community, giving guidance and try to ensure the locals won’t to ostracise them.

The other very nice sequence is with the Ebola task–force nutritionist, a striking and smiley lady who prepares the provisions for quarantined households. She engagingly takes us through all the items pointing out the ones they have run out if already.

Supplies are low.
